# Target Personas

> **Instructions:** Define your target personas below. The AI scorer uses these to classify each LinkedIn connection. Add or remove personas as needed. Replace all [BRACKETS] with your specific information.

---

## Persona 1: Decision Maker (DM)

**Role patterns:** VP of [DEPARTMENT], Director of [DEPARTMENT], Head of [DEPARTMENT], Chief [ROLE] Officer, SVP [DEPARTMENT]

**Company characteristics:** [INDUSTRY] companies with [SIZE RANGE — e.g., "100–2,000 employees"]. Typically have [CONTEXT — e.g., "a dedicated operations or analytics function"]. Annual revenue [RANGE — e.g., "$10M–$500M"].

**Why they matter:** These are the people who sign off on purchases and own the budget. They feel the pain of [PROBLEM YOUR PRODUCT SOLVES] directly and have the authority to approve a solution. They are your primary buying contact.

**Fit score guidance:** 80–100. These connections are high-priority. They should always be kept and nurtured in your network.

---

## Persona 2: Technical Champion

**Role patterns:** Senior [ROLE], Lead [ROLE], [DEPARTMENT] Manager, Staff [ROLE], Principal [ROLE], Solutions Architect

**Company characteristics:** Same company profile as Decision Maker. These individuals work in [DEPARTMENT — e.g., "engineering, data, or IT"] and are hands-on with [RELEVANT TOOLS/PROCESSES — e.g., "the systems your product integrates with"].

**Why they matter:** Champions evaluate tools, run proof-of-concept trials, and advocate internally for adoption. Even without budget authority, their recommendation often determines the outcome. They translate technical value into business justification for the DM.

**Fit score guidance:** 65–79. Strong connections worth keeping. They may not buy directly, but they influence the decision and can become long-term advocates.

## Persona 3: Influencer / Community Voice

**Role patterns:** [ROLE — e.g., "Consultant"], Advisor, Analyst, [INDUSTRY] Thought Leader, Community Manager, Podcast Host, Newsletter Author, Founder of [RELEVANT COMMUNITY]

**Company characteristics:** Often at [TYPE — e.g., "consulting firms, advisory practices, media companies, or independent"]. May not match your target company profile, but they reach an audience that does.

**Why they matter:** Influencers shape perception in your market. A positive mention, referral, or co-created content piece can open doors that cold outreach cannot. They are valuable for brand awareness and credibility, not direct revenue.

**Fit score guidance:** 50–64. Worth keeping selectively. Prioritize those who are active in your target market and have genuine reach. Deprioritize inactive or off-topic connections.
